# 📊 Player Journey Analysis – Key Insights

## Overview

Using the Player Journey Visualization Tool, I analyzed player movement, combat events, loot distribution, and match progression across multiple matches. The goal was to identify actionable patterns that can help level designers improve engagement, balance, and overall gameplay experience.

---

## 🔍 Insight 1: Player Activity is Highly Concentrated in Specific Zones (Hotspots)

**What caught my eye**
Movement heatmaps and loot markers show that players consistently converge in a few regions of the map, especially early in the match.

**Evidence**

* Dense clustering in movement heatmap
* High frequency of loot pickups in the same zones
* Overlapping player paths during early-to-mid match timeline

**Actionable Takeaways**

* Redistribute loot more evenly across the map
* Introduce incentives (objectives, rare resources) in low-traffic areas
* Randomize high-value loot spawn locations

**Metrics Impacted**

* Session length ↑
* Map exploration rate ↑
* Early-game death rate ↓

**Why a Level Designer Should Care**
This indicates that current map design is unintentionally funneling players into predictable areas. Addressing this improves map balance and creates more varied gameplay experiences.

---

## 🔍 Insight 2: Large Portions of the Map are Underutilized (Dead Zones)

**What caught my eye**
Several areas of the map show little to no movement or events, indicating players are avoiding these regions.

**Evidence**

* Minimal or zero heatmap density in certain zones
* Lack of loot interactions and combat events
* No visible player paths through these areas

**Actionable Takeaways**

* Add high-value loot or unique resources in these zones
* Improve accessibility (terrain/navigation changes)
* Introduce environmental cues or landmarks

**Metrics Impacted**

* Map utilization ↑
* Player engagement ↑
* Exploration diversity ↑

**Why a Level Designer Should Care**
Unused areas reduce the effective size and value of the map. Improving these zones leads to better spatial balance and richer gameplay.

---

## 🔍 Insight 3: Combat is Clustered Around Loot-Dense Areas (Choke Points)

**What caught my eye**
Kill and death events are concentrated around areas with high loot density, creating predictable combat zones.

**Evidence**

* Overlap between loot markers and kill events
* Combat occurring shortly after loot interactions
* Repeated clustering of events in the same regions

**Actionable Takeaways**

* Distribute high-tier loot across multiple regions
* Introduce delayed or dynamic loot spawning
* Balance risk vs reward across zones

**Metrics Impacted**

* Combat distribution balance ↑
* Early elimination rate ↓
* Match fairness ↑

**Why a Level Designer Should Care**
Concentrated combat zones create repetitive gameplay and can frustrate players. Better distribution leads to more strategic and balanced encounters.

---

## 🎯 Final Takeaway

The current gameplay patterns suggest that:

* Players optimize for high-reward zones
* Movement and combat are predictable
* Certain areas are consistently ignored

By redistributing resources and improving map incentives, designers can:

* Increase engagement
* Improve replayability
* Create more balanced and dynamic gameplay experiences
